# Standard Operating Procedure: Textile Export Audit and Compliance

**SOP ID:** AUD-TEX-001  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** October 26, 2023  
**Owner:** Quality Assurance & Compliance Department  

---

## 1. Purpose
This SOP establishes a standardized process for auditing textile export shipments. The objective is to ensure 100% data integrity between financial documents and physical goods, maintain 100% regulatory compliance with international trade laws, and mitigate financial risks associated with export discrepancies.

## 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all export-bound textile shipments, covering the period from the generation of the Purchase Order (PO) to the final release of the shipment to the carrier. It involves the Logistics, Finance, and Compliance departments.

---

## 3. Step-by-Step Procedure

### Step 1: Document Collection and Centralization
*   **Owner:** Logistics Coordinator
*   **Action:** Gather the "Export Document Set" (PO, Commercial Invoice, Packing List, Bill of Lading/Airway Bill, Certificate of Origin, and Export License).
*   **Measurable Thresholds:**
    *   **Timeline:** All documents must be centralized in the Audit Dossier within **4 business hours** of shipment booking.
    *   **Completeness:** 100% match against the "Master Document Checklist."
*   **Decision Gate:** 
    *   If any document is missing: Issue a "Document Request" via the ERP system. The audit is paused until the file is uploaded.
    *   If all documents are present: Proceed to Step 2.

### Step 2: Invoice Verification against Purchase Orders
*   **Owner:** Finance Auditor
*   **Action:** Cross-reference line items on the Commercial Invoice against the original PO.
*   **Measurable Thresholds:**
    *   **Fabric Specifications:** GSM (Grams per Square Meter) and fiber content must match exactly.
    *   **Quantity/Weight Tolerance:** Discrepancies must not exceed **+/- 5%** of the PO volume.
    *   **Financials:** Unit prices and currency must have **0% variance**.
    *   **Incoterms:** Must match the PO (e.g., FOB, CIF, DDP).
*   **Decision Gate:**
    *   If variance > 5% or price mismatch: Flag as "Financial Discrepancy" and move to Step 4.
    *   If within tolerance: Proceed to Step 3.

### Step 3: Export Regulation Compliance Check
*   **Owner:** Compliance Officer
*   **Action:** Validate HS (Harmonized System) Codes and trade agreement eligibility.
*   **Measurable Thresholds:**
    *   **HS Code Accuracy:** Must match the current year’s WCO (World Customs Organization) tariff schedule to the **8-digit level**.
    *   **FTA Rules:** If claiming preferential duty, the "Rule of Origin" must be verified against the specific Free Trade Agreement (FTA) text.
    *   **Quota Check:** Shipment volume must be within the remaining allocated export quota for the destination country.
*   **Decision Gate:**
    *   If HS Code is incorrect or Quota is exceeded: Flag as "Compliance Violation" and move to Step 4.
    *   If compliant: Proceed to Step 6 (skipping 4 and 5).

### Step 4: Discrepancy Identification and Reporting
*   **Owner:** Lead Auditor
*   **Action:** Categorize and log all flags from Steps 2 and 3 into the Interim Audit Findings report.
*   **Measurable Thresholds:**
    *   **Minor:** Typographical errors, minor weight variance (<5%).
    *   **Major:** Incorrect Incoterms, missing certificates, weight variance (>5%).
    *   **Critical:** Fraudulent data, illegal HS Code, restricted destination.
    *   **Reporting Time:** Interim report must be published within **2 hours** of discrepancy identification.
*   **Decision Gate:**
    *   If "Critical": Immediate "Stop Shipment" order issued; escalate to Executive Management within **15 minutes**.
    *   If "Minor/Major": Proceed to Step 5.

### Step 5: Corrective Action Follow-up
*   **Owner:** Relevant Department Head (Finance or Logistics)
*   **Action:** Execute corrections (e.g., re-issuing invoices, updating packing lists).
*   **Measurable Thresholds:**
    *   **Resolution Time (Major):** Must be resolved within **24 hours**.
    *   **Resolution Time (Minor):** Must be resolved within **48 hours**.
    *   **Verification:** Auditor must perform a "Spot Re-Check" on the specific corrected field.
*   **Decision Gate:**
    *   If risk is neutralized: Proceed to Step 6.
    *   If risk persists: Re-open discrepancy and return to Step 4.

### Step 6: Final Audit Report Submission and Release
*   **Owner:** Export Manager
*   **Action:** Compile all logs and evidence into the Final Audit Report.
*   **Measurable Thresholds:**
    *   **Archival:** Digital copy of the Audit Dossier must be uploaded to the secure server with a **7-year retention tag**.
    *   **Approval:** Report must be signed by the Export Manager.
*   **Decision Gate:**
    *   **Export Release:** Shipment is cleared for departure only if the Final Audit Report status is "Certified Compliant."

---

## 4. Exceptions
*   **Emergency Shipments:** In cases of documented "Line Down" situations at the customer site, Step 2 and 3 may be performed concurrently to save time, but neither can be bypassed.
*   **System Downtime:** If the ERP is offline for >4 hours, manual paper-based checklists must be used, with data back-entered within 24 hours of system restoration.
*   **Sample Shipments:** Shipments with a total commercial value under **$500 USD** are exempt from the full audit but must still undergo the HS Code verification in Step 3.
